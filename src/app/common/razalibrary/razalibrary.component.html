Raza Library Moradabad

Moradabad

The Rampur Raza Library (R훮mpur Raz훮 Kit훮b Kh훮na) located in Rampur, Uttar Pradesh, India is a repository of Indo-Islamic cultural heritage and a treasure-house of knowledge established in the last decades of the 18th century. It was built up by successive Nawabs of Rampur and is now managed by the Government of India on the name of Raza Ali Khan of Rampur.

After Rampur State joined the Union of India, in 1949 the Library was controlled by the management of a Trust which was created on 6 August 1951. The Trust management continued until July 1975. Prof. S. Nurul Hasan, Minister for Education, Social Welfare and Culture of the Govt. of India, came repeatedly to the Library and took a serious view of the neglected condition of this precious heritage.

How To Reach

By Air

The nearest working domestic airport is Pant Nagar City, 86 km away, the second airport is Dehradun Airport. Muradabad is 175 km away from the Dehradun Airport (DED), Dehradun, Uttarakhand. IGI Delhi is 154 km away. Moradabad airport in Mundapanday is not yet open for domestic traffic.

By Train

Its is less than 1Km away from Moradabad Railway station.

By Road

Moradabad bus stand is 1.3 km from here . Auto and E-Rickshaw are easily available from bus stand and station road.
